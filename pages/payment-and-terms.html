Service Agreement & Terms and Conditions

This Service Agreement (“Agreement”) is entered into between A Team Property Improvement LLC (“Contractor”) and the undersigned client (“Client”). Approval of an estimate, invoice, or work order constitutes acceptance of the following terms.

  1. Scope of Work

    1.1 Services Provided. Contractor agrees to provide labor, materials handling, and related services as described in the attached or referenced estimate, invoice, or work order (collectively, the “Project”).

    1.2 Reliance on Existing Conditions. Estimates are based on visible and disclosed site conditions. Concealed, damaged, unsafe, or inaccessible conditions may require additional work and cost.

    1.3 Scope Changes. Any deviation from the original scope must be approved in writing. Verbal or text-message approvals are considered binding if the Client requests additional work.

  2. Payment Terms

    2.1 Labor Deposit. A non-refundable 50% labor deposit is required before scheduling or beginning work.

    2.2 Materials. All materials must be paid in advance, supplied by the Client, or reimbursed at the time of purchase.

    2.3 Final Balance. The remaining labor balance is due upon normal completion of the Project.

    2.4 Payment Deadlines (Net 15). All invoices are due within 15 days of the invoice date (Net 15).

    2.5 Late Fees & Interest. Invoices not paid within 15 days will incur: 1.5% interest per month (18% APR) on outstanding balances. Contractor may suspend work until all past-due balances are paid.

    2.6 Collections. Client agrees to pay all reasonable costs of collection, including administrative fees, attorney fees, and court costs if required.

  3. Client Responsibilities

    3.1 Access. Client must provide reasonable access to the property, including keys, codes, utilities, parking, and clear pathways.

    3.2 Safety & Site Conditions. Client must ensure the work area is safe and free of hazards. Contractor is not responsible for delays caused by unsafe conditions.

    3.3 Property Protection. Client must remove or protect personal belongings from the workspace. Contractor is not liable for damage to unprotected items in or near the work zone.

    3.4 Approvals. Client must promptly approve materials, selections, and change orders to avoid delays.

  4. Contractor Responsibilities

    4.1 Workmanship. Contractor will perform all work in a professional and workmanlike manner.

    4.2 Compliance. Contractor will comply with applicable laws, codes, and safety regulations.

    4.3 Insurance. Contractor maintains general liability and workers’ compensation insurance as required.

  5. Change Orders & Additional Work

    5.1 Definition. A Change Order includes any work not clearly listed in the approved estimate.

    5.2 Unforeseen Conditions. These include, but are not limited to, rot, mold, termite damage, structural issues, outdated or damaged wiring or plumbing, code-related upgrades, and inaccessible components.

    5.3 Approval. All additional work must be approved in writing. For time-sensitive or on-site requests, Client approval via text message is sufficient.

    5.4 Billing Rate. Unless otherwise specified, additional work will be billed at: $65/hour for labor, plus cost of materials.

  6. Warranties & Limitations

    6.1 Labor Warranty. Contractor warrants its labor for 90 days from completion against defects in workmanship.

    6.2 Exclusions. Warranty does not cover: normal wear and tear; misuse, neglect, or unauthorized repairs; issues arising from existing property defects; damage caused by weather or environmental factors; failures caused by Client-provided materials.

    6.3 Materials Warranty. Manufacturer warranties apply directly to materials. Contractor does not warrant Client-supplied or discounted materials.

    6.4 Limitation of Liability. Contractor is not liable for incidental, indirect, consequential, or punitive damages, including but not limited to: loss of use; delay-related losses; pre-existing structural or mechanical issues; water, mold, or rot issues not caused by Contractor.

  7. Media Use & Documentation

    7.1 Site Documentation. Contractor may photograph or record the Project for documentation, quality control, and liability protection.

    7.2 Promotional Use. Client grants Contractor permission to use jobsite photos and videos for marketing, advertising, and portfolio purposes.

    7.3 Privacy Measures. All identifying personal information (addresses, license plates, personal items, faces) will be blurred or removed.

    7.4 Waiver. Client waives the right to compensation or approval of final media.

  8. Termination

    8.1 Termination by Either Party. Either party may terminate this Agreement with written notice.

    8.2 Payment on Termination. Client must pay for: all labor performed up to termination; all materials purchased or ordered; any non-cancelable material or vendor costs; reasonable charges for schedule disruption or mobilization.

    8.3 Right to Suspend Work. Contractor may suspend work at any time for non-payment, unsafe conditions, harassment, or unreasonable demands.

  9. Entire Agreement

    This Agreement, together with the approved estimate or work order, constitutes the entire understanding between Contractor and Client and supersedes all prior discussions, verbal or written. Amendments must be made in writing and acknowledged by both parties.
